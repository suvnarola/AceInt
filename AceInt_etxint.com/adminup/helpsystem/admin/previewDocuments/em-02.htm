Introduction

This document is designed to assist you in communicating with large ammounts of your E Banc Trade membership via Email. Before you start to do this, you should read about SPAM. This document was written as a tutorial to guide you through the process step by step. The Best way to read this is from start to finish, following all instructions and taking note of the information presented.

Spam

One of the greatest issues facing digital communications these days is SPAM. Chances are that if you have been using email for a while, you will have received emails that you don't want or need.  These emails are called spam. In most countries there is no law against this.  In Australia however there are strict Anti-SPAM laws. We at E Banc Trade fully comply with these laws and if you are sending out bulk emails you will need to as well.

Privacy, Spam and E Banc Trade

As part of our membership approval process, members of E Banc Trade are protected by our Privacy Policy which includes information on how we deal with SPAM.  This information is available from our corporate website.

 Our policy strictly prohibits unsolicited, commercial email (spam).  Under no circumstances is a member's personal information to be disclosed to a third party without the approval of head office, and the consent of the member. To further comply with privacy regulations, the email addresses of members must not be displayed to other members when communicating with multiple email addresses.

To comply with the SPAM Act, all emails to multiple recipients must include an unsubscribe paragraph, and must identify the sender's company, address and contact details (this information is included in your E Banc Trade email stationery).  In order to comply fully with the SPAM Act, all communication to E Banc Trade members must be regarding E Banc Trade business or member services..

Preparing Email Content

All bulk emails that you send out should be on your E Banc Trade stationary or on a specialised E Banc Trade newsletter template (help on setting up your newsletter template is available here).Only send out an email publication on your E Banc Trade email address ([yourName]@ebanctrade.com) if you do not have access to an E Banc Trade address please contact Head Office and one will be provided.

No matter what your content is, you always need to put the following paragraph at the end of the email to comply with australian spam law.

This message is sent to online members of E Banc Trade. If you
feel that your email was obtained by error, or you no longer wish
to receive membership promotions, please click here and hit send

Retrieving your List of Email Addresses

Now you need to get the most up to date list of email addresses for your membership base.

Open the Intranet website and select ‘List Centre’ from the left hand navigation table under ‘Admin’.  Your licensed area/s will be listed.  You must select your area (even if only one displays) to select it.  If you have more than one area and you want the email addresses for all of them.  Click on the first, then holding down the ‘Shift’ key, click on the last area.  They should all then be highlighted.  By holding down the ‘Control’ key you can select multiple, but non-consecutive, areas.

If you are sending out information about Real Estate opportunities only, tick the box.  Members can elect not to receive this type of promotion. 
You also have to option to retrieve email addresses for sponsorships only by selecting the ‘Sponsorships’ button.

If you want all email addresses for online members in your area, simple select the ‘Email List’ button, after highlighting the areas you are choosing to receive.

You will receive and email with a text attachment containing all your member email addresses.

Inserting your Email Addresses and Sending your Email

Now you are ready to address your email.
Open the email you received earlier.  The Subject will be ‘Updated Email List’.  Open the attachment by double clicking on it.  You will see a page with email addresses on it, separated by a semi colon (;). 

Put your cursor at the beginning of the text, and holding the left mouse button down, drag the cursor to the end of the last email address.  They should all then be highlighted.  While holding down the ‘Control’ key, press the ‘C’ key. Alternately, select ‘Edit’ from the text toolbar at the top, then ‘Copy’.

Now go back to your open email message that you have just created and click on the ‘To’ button and the ‘Select Names’ window will open.

Position your cursor by clicking inside the field box to the right of the ‘Bcc’ button, then holding down the ‘Control’ key on your keyboard, press the ‘V’ key.  This should paste all the email addresses you previously copied into the Bcc (Blind carbon copy) field box.

Position your cursor by clicking inside the field box to the right of the ‘To’ button. Then type a name for the group of members that you are sending to followed by a less-than sign (<), your email and a greater-than sign (>). eg. E Banc Traders - Sunshine Coast <hq@ebanctrade.com>.

Your email should now look like this.  All that is left to do is spell check the message, then hit send.
